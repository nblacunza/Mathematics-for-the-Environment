Solving Nonlinear Equations

This chapter focuses on finding roots of equations where an exact algebraic solution is impossible—a fundamental challenge in practical applications.

"The characters of the universe are written in the language of mathematics." Explore how we translate these characters into Julia code.

Why Julia?

Numerical Methods Approach

In climate science, we very often need to find where a function crosses zero. Understanding numerical root-finding methods is therefore essential.

Didactic Note: Every algorithm presented here balances three key factors: robustness (will it find the root?), speed (how many steps?), and requirements (do we need the derivative?).

Iterative Methods

Picard Iteration

A fixed-point iteration method based on rewriting the equation as x = g(x). Simple, intuitive, and foundational for convergence theory.

Explore Method →

Secant Method

An approximation that uses two points to draw a line. Faster than Picard iteration and doesn't require calculating derivatives analytically.

Explore Method →

Newton-Raphson

The gold standard for speed. By using the tangent at a point, it could achieve quadratic convergence toward the solution.

Explore Method →
