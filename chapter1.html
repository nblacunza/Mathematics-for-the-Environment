Iterative Methods

Picard Iteration

A fixed-point iteration method based on rewriting the equation as x = g(x). Simple, intuitive, and foundational for convergence theory.

Explore Method →

Secant Method

An approximation that uses two points to draw a line. Faster than fixed-point iteration and avoids explicit derivatives.

Newton-Raphson

The gold standard for speed. By using the tangent at a point, it achieves quadratic convergence toward the solution.
